This single-page resource helps you identify household hazardous waste, learn proper disposal methods, and find example drop-off locations across the US with GPS coordinates.

Batteries

Description: Contain heavy metals like lead, cadmium, and mercury.

Disposal: Recycle at stores (e.g., Best Buy) or HHW facilities. Never in trash.

Paints & Solvents

Description: Oil-based paints, thinners, and varnishes contain VOCs.

Disposal: Take to HHW collection; latex paint can sometimes be dried and trashed (check local rules).

Household Cleaners

Description: Bleach, ammonia, drain openers – corrosive or toxic.

Disposal: HHW facility only. Never mix or pour down drain.

Pesticides & Herbicides

Description: Highly toxic to humans, pets, and waterways.

Disposal: HHW events or permanent facilities only.

Motor Oil & Antifreeze

Description: Contaminates soil and water if dumped.

Disposal: Recycle at auto parts stores or HHW sites.

Fluorescent Bulbs & CFLs

Description: Contain small amounts of mercury.

Disposal: Recycle at Home Depot, Lowe's, or HHW.

Electronics (E-Waste)

Description: TVs, computers, phones contain lead and other toxins.

Disposal: Certified e-waste recyclers or retailer take-back programs.

Medications

Description: Can contaminate water if flushed.

Disposal: DEA take-back events, police stations, or participating pharmacies.
