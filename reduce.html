Reduce Household Hazardous Waste (HHW)

"Ang pagbabawas ay mas mabuti kaysa tamang pagtatapon."

The best way to manage hazardous waste is to prevent it from being generated in the first place. Reducing the purchase and use of hazardous products, choosing safer alternatives, and proper storage can significantly decrease HHW in your home while complying with Philippine laws like RA 6969 and RA 9003.

These simple steps protect your family, pets, and the environment while saving money.

General Tips to Reduce HHW

Buy Only What You Need

  • Purchase smaller quantities to avoid leftovers.
  • Measure accurately for paints, pesticides, etc.
  • Share large containers with neighbors or friends.

Use Products Completely

  • Follow label instructions for full use.
  • Donate usable leftovers (e.g., paint) to community programs.
  • Store properly to extend shelf life.

Choose Safer Products

  • Look for "non-toxic" or eco-friendly labels.
  • Opt for plant-based or natural cleaners available locally.
  • Avoid products with strong warning labels like "Danger" or "Poison".

Proper Storage

  • Keep in original containers with labels.
  • Store in cool, dry places away from children/pets.
  • Prevent spills and reactions.

Safer Natural Alternatives

Common natural ingredients like baking soda, vinegar (suka), and lemon can replace many hazardous cleaners. These are affordable, effective, and widely recommended for Philippine households.

Common Hazardous Product Safer Alternative How to Use
All-Purpose Cleaner White Vinegar + Water Mix equal parts; spray and wipe surfaces.
Drain Cleaner Baking Soda + Vinegar Pour ½ cup baking soda, then ½ cup vinegar; wait 15 min, flush with hot water.
Oven Cleaner Baking Soda Paste Sprinkle baking soda + water; let sit overnight, scrub.
Window Cleaner Vinegar + Water Mix 1:4 ratio; use newspaper for streak-free shine.
Air Freshener Baking Soda or Open Windows Place open box in room or ventilate naturally.
Furniture Polish Olive Oil + Lemon Juice Mix 2:1; apply with soft cloth.
Sources: DENR-EMB guidelines on hazardous waste management (RA 6969 & RA 9003); common eco-friendly cleaning practices recommended in the Philippines; inspired by international best practices including U.S. EPA.
